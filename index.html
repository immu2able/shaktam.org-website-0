Welcome to Shaktam.org

Shaktam or Shaktism (/ˈsæktɪzəm/; Sanskrit: शाक्तसम्प्रदायः, romanized: Śāktasampradāyaḥ) is a major Hindu denomination in which the deity or metaphysical reality is considered metaphorically to be a woman.


Śāktasampradāyaḥ involves a galaxy of goddesses, all being regarded as different aspects, manifestations, or personifications of the divine feminine energy called Shakti. It consists of various modes of worship, ranging from those focused on the most worshipped Durga, to gracious Parvati, and the fierce Kali. The most common forms of the Devis worshipped in Shaktism include: Durga, Kali, Saraswati, Lakshmi, Parvati, and Maha Tripurasundari.
